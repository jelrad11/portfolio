"Kingdom" game concept

"Fox in a Box" was my secondary school graduation project with which I aimed to introduce the reader to the intrigues of game development, specifically a 2D isometric game. On 71 pages the reader could find my game development thought processes, details on programming, designing mechanics, etc. supplemented with a dictionary explaining some terms. You can read more about the game itself below.

Concept

Fox K. is a game about escaping. The character, Fox K. works at an unspecified corporation, doing mundane and tiring office job, never leaving for home and always working. He lives in his small cubicle, surrounded by papers and "eats" nothing but coffee. One day something snaps in him and he decides to leave no matter what. But how? All the doors are locked and closely watched and the office has no windows whatsoever.

As the player is trying to find a way out, asking around the office for help and bonding with others to save himself, while also fulfilling the assignments from his boss, he learns to cope with the struggles and tries to do his best to make the environment more liveable, thus altering his objective from escaping to changing the work environment. This is one of the potential endings, there are still other ways to escape like the player is allowed to escape through vent shaft at one point in the game if they desire so, but its only after becoming friends with another coworker, that they'll have to leave behind. Once the player leaves the office site the game ends.

This game was heavily influenced by The Escapists, game about escaping a prison and bonding with other prison mates.
