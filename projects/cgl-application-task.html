Fire Edge

CGL application task 2020

Radim Jelínek

Disclaimer: This page has not been edited after 31st of March. Everything had been created within the time designated for the task.

Fire Edge is a 3D sidescroller RPG and a turn-based strategy game targeted for mobile devices, offering an insight into a potential dystopian future where the world is engulfed in flames and the last of creatures are fighting for their lives. Its target audience is teens to young adults.

Assume the role of a character fighting their way through a burning world, make friends and save the planet! Fire Edge is about survival.

Summary

Players can occasionally stumble on tools placed in the world. These objects, if found and collected, can help users get through puzzle levels. They are not limited by use, but there's a recharge time on them. For example, if the player was to use an axe to cut down a tree, they would have to wait 2 rounds until the axe can be used again.

Tools are divided into three categories: Red ("Destructive"; e.g. Axe, Dynamite), Blue ("Defensive"; Watering can, Fire extinguisher) and Yellow("Special tools"; e.g. inflatable boat)

Player can have only 3 objects equipped at a time, one Special tool at maximum. These can be selected in the character menu.

It's not expected from the player to find all the objects the game has to offer. Some have the same perks and differ only in appearance (axe, handsaw) This way each player can experience the game differently, offering them a diverse pool of tools to pick from.

Controls

As this game is targeted for mobile devices, I tried to make it as easy to control as possible. Players can move their character by either touching the edge of their screen (character moves left or right) or by clicking on the ground (both left and right with additional option to move closer/farther from the camera). Both options can be enabled/disable in the game's menu. In both cases the player can click on tools placed in the 3D world, the character will move towards them and the camera changes to a close-up shot.

Written Assignment

Hieroglyphs of the future can be interpreted as a well-thought-out reflection of our time in which an influential innovation has prompted humankind to alter the world.

Due to climate change, depicting a dystopian world scenario where the planet is engulfed in flames seems to be the most fitting for my game. As uncontrolled wildfires are one of today’s top concerns that could potentially result in civilisation-wide catastrophe (pg10), this game aims to express the undesirable outcomes that can potentially await us (pg15). It could be interpreted as a wake-up call to change our ways before it’s too late (pg16).
